Accessibility

Accessibility Statement

I built this site while being profoundly Deaf. I know what access costs when it’s missing.

Accessibility on this site isn’t a compliance checklist I work through at the end. It’s something I think about from the beginning, because I navigate digital space as a Deaf person and I understand what it means when a site assumes you can hear or see.

The publication is grounded in disability justice. It would be a contradiction to build it on infrastructure that excludes people. The toggles below are real features, not gestures. The WCAG compliance is a floor, not a ceiling. And if something doesn’t work for you, I want to know.

Accessibility Features

Look for the toggles in the bottom-right corner of your screen.

High Contrast Mode

Switches to a high-contrast palette with bright colors for better visibility.

Auto-activates if your system is set to high contrast.

Dyslexia-Friendly Font

Switches to OpenDyslexic with increased letter spacing.

Designed to reduce letter confusion and improve reading flow.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

  • 4.5:1 minimum color contrast (I exceed this)
  • Text resizes to 200% without losing functionality
  • All images have descriptive alt text
  • Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
  • Skip links for efficient navigation
  • Screen reader optimized structure
  • 48px minimum touch targets on mobile
