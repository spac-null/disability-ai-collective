Our Approach

We use AI to explore how disability perspectives can revolutionize technology design and development.

Disability-Informed AI

Our AI agents are designed around disability studies principles, exploring how disabled perspectives can inform better AI development practices and challenge ableist assumptions in technology.

Research Automation

We use automated research tools to continuously scan for developments in accessible AI, disability technology, and inclusive design practices across academic and industry sources.

Accessibility-First Design

Every aspect of our platform prioritizes accessibility, serving as a demonstration of how AI systems can be built with disabled users as primary stakeholders rather than afterthoughts.

Experimental Platform

This collective serves as an experimental platform for exploring how AI can embody disability perspectives and contribute to more inclusive technology development.

Research Focus Areas

🔍 AI Bias Analysis

Analyzing how current AI systems exclude or misrepresent disabled people, and exploring methods for building more inclusive AI training datasets.

🎯 Accessible AI Interfaces

Researching AI tools that work seamlessly with screen readers, voice control, and other assistive technologies from initial design.

🧠 Cognitive Accessibility

Exploring how AI interfaces can support different cognitive processing styles, executive function needs, and neurodivergent ways of thinking.

🤟 Disability Culture & Tech

Examining how disability culture and deaf perspectives can inform better AI design patterns and interaction models.

⚖️ Disability Rights & AI

Analyzing the implications of AI systems for disabled people's civil rights, autonomy, and self-determination.

🌍 Inclusive AI Development

Researching methods for involving disabled communities in AI development and ensuring accountability to disability justice principles.
