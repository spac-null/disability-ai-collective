Our Approach

We believe disability perspectives aren't accommodations to add later—they're innovations that benefit everyone.

Disability Justice Framework

Our work is grounded in disability justice principles: leadership by those most impacted, intersectionality, anti-capitalism, and collective liberation. We reject the medical model that sees disability as something to be "fixed" by technology.

Research Ethics

Every research project includes disabled community members as co-investigators, not subjects. We prioritize community benefit over commercial applications and share all findings openly.

Visual-Spatial Excellence

Deaf researchers bring visual-spatial processing strengths that revolutionize AI interface design. Our patterns emphasize spatial relationships, visual hierarchies, and gestural interactions that hearing-centric design often misses.

Nothing About Us, Without Us

This disability rights principle guides everything we do. Disabled people lead our research, review our findings, and shape our priorities. We're accountable to our communities first.

Research Focus Areas

🔍 AI Bias & Representation

Documenting how current AI systems exclude or misrepresent disabled people. Building more inclusive training datasets and evaluation metrics.

🎯 Accessible AI Interfaces

Designing AI tools that work for screen readers, voice control, eye tracking, and other assistive technologies from day one.

🧠 Cognitive Accessibility

Researching how AI can support different cognitive processing styles, executive function needs, and neurodivergent ways of thinking.

🤟 Sign Language AI

Advancing computer vision for sign language recognition and generation, led by deaf researchers who understand the linguistic complexity.

⚖️ Disability Rights & Tech

Analyzing the legal and policy implications of AI systems for disabled people's civil rights and self-determination.

🌍 Global Disability Perspectives

Collaborating with disabled researchers worldwide to ensure our work reflects diverse cultural approaches to disability and technology.
