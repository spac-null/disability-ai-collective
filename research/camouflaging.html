Autism Camouflaging

The clinical literature calls it a social competency. It is not. It is a resource allocation problem — and the research measured the output while never instrumenting the cost.

Autistic people — disproportionately women, and those diagnosed late or not at all — learn to produce socially legible behaviour through sustained effort that does not diminish with practice. The literature named this camouflaging and classified it as a skill. Below is Zen Circuit's analysis of what the research actually measured, why the classification is wrong, and what the failure mode looks like when the cost comes due.
