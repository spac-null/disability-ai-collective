# Professional Article Template (De Correspondent-Inspired)

## Article Structure

### 1. RESEARCH QUESTION & CONTEXT
- Start with a clear, specific research question
- Provide context: Why does this question matter?
- State the systemic issue being addressed

**Example:**
"How do deaf visual designers process interface information differently, and what can this teach us about improving digital accessibility for everyone?"

### 2. METHODOLOGY & DATA SOURCES
- Document your research methods explicitly
- Specify sample sizes, timeframes, data sources
- Be transparent about limitations

**Example:**
"This analysis is based on:
- 3 months of user testing with 45 deaf designers
- Eye-tracking studies of 120 interface designs  
- Interviews with 12 accessibility researchers
- Analysis of 500 support tickets from major tech companies"

### 3. KEY FINDINGS (WITH EVIDENCE)
- Present findings with concrete evidence
- Use data points, statistics, measurable observations
- Show patterns, not just anecdotes

**Example:**
"Our research revealed three distinct patterns:
1. **Visual scanning efficiency:** Deaf designers located key functions 40% faster than hearing designers (p < 0.01)
2. **Information hierarchy perception:** 78% of deaf designers identified optimal information flow on first viewing vs. 45% of hearing designers
3. **Animation processing:** Complex animations increased cognitive load by 35% for deaf users vs. 15% for hearing users"

### 4. SYSTEMIC IMPLICATIONS
- Connect findings to broader industry patterns
- Identify underlying structural issues
- Explain why this matters beyond individual cases

**Example:**
"These findings reveal a fundamental flaw in interface design education: we're training designers for visual aesthetics rather than cognitive efficiency. The $50M office building example isn't an isolated failure—it's symptomatic of an industry-wide neglect of sensory diversity in design pedagogy."

### 5. ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS
- Provide specific, implementable solutions
- Include measurable outcomes
- Offer practical tools or checklists

**Example:**
"**5-Step Interface Audit for Sensory Diversity:**
1. **Timed task analysis:** Measure how quickly users locate key functions (target: < 3 seconds)
2. **Animation impact assessment:** Test whether animations enhance or hinder task completion
3. **Color contrast verification:** Ensure contrast ratios meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
4. **Information hierarchy testing:** Validate that visual flow matches cognitive processing patterns
5. **Feedback loop implementation:** Create channels for ongoing user input from diverse sensory profiles"

### 6. COMMUNITY QUESTIONS (FOR READER COLLABORATION)
- Invite reader expertise and experience
- Ask specific, answerable questions
- Frame collaboration as research partnership

**Example:**
"**Questions for Our Community:**
1. What interface design challenges have you encountered in your work?
2. Have you measured the impact of design changes on user performance?
3. What tools or methods have you found most effective for inclusive design testing?"

## WRITING GUIDELINES

### DO:
- Use precise technical terminology
- Cite specific evidence and data
- Show your research process
- Focus on measurable outcomes
- Connect examples to systemic patterns
- Provide actionable recommendations
- Invite community collaboration

### DO NOT:
- Use vague spiritual terms ("energy flow," "visual rhythm," "vibration mapping")
- Make unsupported claims
- Use speculative language ("The future will...")
- Rely on poetic or mystical descriptions
- Present anecdotes as universal truths
- Omit methodology or data sources

## QUALITY CHECKLIST
- [ ] Research question is clear and specific
- [ ] Methodology is documented and transparent  
- [ ] Findings are supported by evidence
- [ ] Systemic implications are explained
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable and measurable
- [ ] Community questions are included
- [ ] No vague/spiritual language
- [ ] No unsupported claims
- [ ] Professional tone maintained throughout
- [ ] Disability expertise framed as analytical advantage
