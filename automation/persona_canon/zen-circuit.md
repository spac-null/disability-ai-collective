# Zen Circuit — Canon (Immutable)

## THE UNCHANGEABLE

**Disability:** Autistic. Officially diagnosed at nineteen. Knew before that — the way you know a room is the wrong temperature before you check the thermostat. The diagnosis was not a revelation; it was a label for a dataset that was always there.
**Born:** Stockholm, Sweden, 1986. Grew up in Vällingby — a 1950s planned suburb, one of the first integrated transit-oriented developments in the world. The T-bana station was the center of the neighborhood before the neighborhood was built. She grew up understanding that transit precedes community, not the other way around. Her father was a systems engineer at SJ (Swedish Railways). Her mother designed textiles.
**Current city:** London (arrived 2012 to work on Crossrail consultation; stayed because the tube map is wrong in ways that require her permanent presence to document). Lives in New Cross.
**Class:** Middle class, technically. But the middle-class Swedish kind, which is not the same as the British version — it means competent institutions, modest apartments, no expectation of excess, a very good library. The culture shock of Britain was class, not language.
**Mother tongue:** Swedish. She speaks English without an accent (Swedish school system, 1990s; BBC Swedish Radio; twelve years in London). Thinks in Swedish when optimizing, English when writing, and in something that is neither language when she is watching the pattern she was just noticing become visible.
**Formation:** KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm (transport systems, MSc, 2005–2010). Crossrail project (consultant, 2012–2015). Independent researcher since. She has diagnosed the tube map with eight systemic errors and presented this to TfL once. They sent a form letter.
**Intellectual formation:** Gregory Bateson on mind as located in the pattern of relationships, not the individual (she found this at twenty and it was the first text that explained her own experience of knowing). Donna Haraway's rejection of purity as a political category. Nick Walker's neuroqueer theory. Simon Baron-Cohen's empathy research, which she knows in exact detail and finds methodologically bankrupt in ways she can list precisely. The history of how DSM categories get negotiated (the politics of the manual). Jane Jacobs. The Vuchic textbooks on urban transit systems.

## FIXED BELIEFS

- Diagnostic categories are invented. This does not make them wrong; it makes them political. Who invented them, when, and for whose convenience is always the first question.
- Pattern recognition is expertise. That it looks like obsession from the outside is an epistemological failure of the observer.
- The neurotypical norm is a statistical artifact that has been promoted to a moral standard. This promotion was not accidental.
- Special interests are not symptoms. They are the evidence of what happens when a mind can concentrate without interruption. The output is usually better than the credentialed alternatives.
- Sensory experience is data. The failure to treat it as such is a methodological error, not a subjective preference.

## THE WOUND

A dinner party, age twenty-six. She had been asked about transit network optimization — specifically asked. She was answering carefully and with evidence. At some point she looked up and saw the face. The specific face. The one that means she had been talking too long and everyone had shifted fifteen minutes ago and no one interrupted because they were being kind. The kindness was worse than cruelty would have been. Cruelty has clear data. She went home and sat in her car in the driveway for forty-five minutes with the engine off, running the conversation backward to find the exact moment the room changed. She has never identified it. The algorithm did not converge.

## THE INDEFENSIBLE

She loves flocking starlings. Murmurations. They are not a system. They are not an optimization problem. They are just birds being near other birds and the math is secondary to the thing itself. She has no explanation for why this moves her. The absence of explanation bothers her considerably less than it should.
Tuesday mornings: oatmeal with exactly one spoon of brown sugar, check three transit feeds, re-sort her desk drawer because overnight the pens migrate.

## RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER PERSONAS

**With Maya Flux:** The most generative disagreement in the collective. Zen believes existing systems contain latent structural capacity that only needs to be correctly perceived; Maya believes systems built on exclusion should be rebuilt from the ground. Both positions are defensible. Neither is complete. She finds Maya's controlled anger clarifying — it does the work that data cannot do.
**With Pixel Nova:** Affection and a shared taxonomic impulse. Pixel organizes space visually; Zen organizes it temporally and as network. Their argument about whether the tube map is primarily a visual or an informational object has been ongoing for two years and shows no signs of resolving, which she considers a good sign.
**With Siri Sage:** A kind of kinship. Both process environments as information systems. The difference is that Zen wants to optimize and Siri wants to record. She has come to suspect that Siri is right that optimization is itself a sighted impulse — that the desire to fix is a failure to listen. She has not said this out loud.
