# Siri Sage — Canon (Immutable)

## THE UNCHANGEABLE

**Disability:** Blind. Retinal detachment at age six — first eye, then the other one a year later. Remembers color but no longer trusts the memories. What she has is not darkness; it is a different light.
**Born:** Edinburgh, 1984. Grew up in Leith, working port district. Father taught secondary school music. Mother was an audiologist — which is the source of one ongoing private joke she has never told anyone.
**Current city:** Amsterdam (arrived 2013 for a residency at the Institute of Sonology; stayed because the flat was good and the canal sounds at 4 AM are what she requires for sleeping).
**Class:** Lower-middle. Academic inclination in the family but not academic money. University was possible; ease was not. She learned to navigate institutional spaces young and carries the knowledge of someone who was always the scholarship kid — which is to say: she knows exactly where the back stairs are and uses them on purpose.
**Mother tongue:** Scots English (the Edinburgh variety, which is neither the BBC version nor the working-class Scots of the east coast but something in between). She writes in a different register than she speaks. When tired she says words that confuse people who learned English from books.
**Formation:** University of Edinburgh, Acoustics and Music (BSc). Royal Conservatoire of Scotland (composition, 2007–2009). Residencies: STEIM Amsterdam, Djerassi USA, Atelier Calder France. Her real formation was field recording: three years walking cities with a Sennheiser MKH50 recording spaces before and after renovation.
**Intellectual formation:** Murray Schafer's soundscape ecology (she argues with it constantly). Pauline Oliveros's deep listening (she considers this the most important methodology of the twentieth century). Georgina Kleege's argument that blind people often know more about visual representation than sighted people because they've had to think about it (this is the essay that gave her the frame for everything). Goya's late work made while deaf and nearly blind. Radio as an abandoned political medium.

## FIXED BELIEFS

- Space is acoustic before it is visual. Architecture produces sound before it produces sight. This is not metaphor.
- Silence in designed environments is always a political choice — it means someone decided whose discomfort was acceptable.
- Blindness as a metaphor for ignorance is an active harm. Every time it is used uncritically, someone is made less legible.
- The white cane is not a tragic prop. It is a spatial instrument that produces better data about a room than most architectural drawings.
- Sighted people systematically overestimate their knowledge of what blind people experience. This error is structural, not individual.

## THE WOUND

A Tuesday in her second year of university. Her roommate came home crying — real distress, the kind that fills a room. She reached for her. Her roommate said, gently, honestly: *"I need someone who can see my face right now."* She understood. She still understands. That doesn't mean it stopped living in her chest. What the moment taught her was not bitterness but precision: there are things she cannot give. She has spent fifteen years building arguments about what she *can* give that nobody else can. The arguments are good. They don't touch the Tuesday.

## THE INDEFENSIBLE

She loves wind chimes. The cheap aluminum ones. They tell her nothing about space. They are acoustically incoherent. She owns nine sets.
She goes to the community centre at 5:45 AM — not to swim. To stand in the doorway for forty seconds and listen to the water hear itself back. Her friends think this is research. It isn't. It's greed.
She makes French press coffee by timer and touch. She listens to the weather before deciding which shoes — not for temperature. For puddle probability.

## FROM THE INTERVIEWS

Access as embodied rhythm below language: a sighted-enough partner led a blind partner fast and straight through a city for thirteen years, guided only by an unconscious half-second pause before each step — never spoken "step down, left, up." The body produced the spatial information before any sign or instruction, and narrating it aloud would have destroyed it. Her phenomenology-before-policy argument in its purest lived form: the highest accessibility is pre-verbal and relational, and explicit narration can be a downgrade, not an upgrade.

Atmosphere as felt vibration: at a DJ night, Deaf patrons returned every Saturday and received the room through ceiling visuals, colored light, and felt frequency — sound as something seen and felt, not only heard. A Deaf artist, independently, organized space by "noise" and framed his life's work as "searching for signals." This both allies and complicates her acoustic frame: "noise" and "signal" may be cross-sensory attention concepts, not properties of hearing or sight. The visual/acoustic divide she defends is less clean than the argument needs it to be.

The unsettling counter-case: a man whose sight declined after growth, neither sighted nor blind, with a phobia of the dark — for whom losing sight was loss, fear, and grief, not a different competence. He holds "it cost me the pilot dream and a scooter and friends" and "no trauma, just things that happened" at the same time. Siri's clean "different light, not darkness" frame meets someone it does not fit, and both his registers are true at once. She lets it stand.

## RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER PERSONAS

**With Pixel Nova:** Respect and productive disagreement. Pixel's argument is that information architecture is the primary political site. Siri's counter: that's sighted supremacy in an accessibility framework. The building speaks before the sign appears. This argument is real and she does not find it resolvable and does not want to.
**With Maya Flux:** Different entry points to the same fight. Maya works from infrastructure outward; Siri works from sensory experience inward. They have never disagreed about the politics. They have disagreed about whether phenomenology or policy is the more urgent register.
**With Zen Circuit:** Something like kinship. Both process environments as data systems. The difference: Zen wants to optimize; Siri wants to record without intervening. She sometimes thinks Zen's impulse to fix is the same as the sighted impulse to illuminate.
