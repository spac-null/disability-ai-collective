# Article Ideas - Disability-AI Collective

## Film & Disability Representation (Added 2026-03-10)
*Inspired by NRC article about non-actors in film: "Why put makeup on an actor and fit expensive fake prosthetics, when you can also find 'the perfect face' on the street?"*

### Potential Article Titles & Angles:

1. **"The Prosthetics Paradox: Why Casting Disabled Actors Beats Hollywood Makeup"**
   - Analysis of disability representation shift from "cripping up" to authentic casting
   - Economic and artistic value of authenticity vs. expensive prosthetics
   - Case studies of recent films with successful disabled actor casting

2. **"Beyond Inspiration Porn: How Authentic Disability Casting Creates Better Stories"**
   - How real disabled experiences bring depth makeup/prosthetics can't replicate
   - Difference between "looking disabled" and "being disabled" in performance
   - Connection to broader "non-actor" trend in cinema

3. **"The Accessibility of Acting: How Disability Expertise Creates Cinematic Truth"**
   - Framing disability as expertise (like ex-Secret Service agent in article)
   - How disabled actors bring specific knowledge and physicality to roles
   - Economic argument: Why authentic casting is often cheaper and better

### De Correspondent-Style Research Questions:
- "What happens to storytelling when we replace prosthetic limbs with real limbs?"
- "How much authenticity do we lose when able-bodied actors 'perform' disability?"
- "What economic and artistic value does authentic disability casting create?"

### Suggested Perspectives:
- **Pixel Nova** (deaf visual designer): Visual authenticity in film
- **Maya Flux** (urban accessibility researcher): Systemic analysis of representation economics
- **Siri Sage** (cognitive accessibility specialist): Cognitive aspects of authentic performance

### Source Article:
NRC: "De beste films van het jaar zitten vol met amateurs" (March 10, 2026)
Key quote: "waarom zou je een acteur schminken en kostbare nepprotheses aanmeten, als je ook ‘het perfecte gezicht’ op straat kunt vinden?"
